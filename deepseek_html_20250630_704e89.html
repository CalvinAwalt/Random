Do you know the calvin formula?

Assistant Response:

The Calvin Formula (Alveolar Gas Equation)

A mathematical equation used in respiratory physiology to estimate the alveolar partial pressure of oxygen (PAO₂).

Standard Formula

PAO₂ = [FIO₂ × (Patm - PH₂O)] - [PACO₂ ÷ R]

Simplified Clinical Formula (Room Air)

PAO₂ = 150 - (PaCO₂ ÷ 0.8)

Variables Explained:

  • PAO₂: Alveolar partial pressure of oxygen (mmHg)
  • FIO₂: Fraction of inspired oxygen (0.21 for room air)
  • Patm: Atmospheric pressure (760 mmHg at sea level)
  • PH₂O: Water vapor pressure (47 mmHg at 37°C)
  • PACO₂: Alveolar partial pressure of CO₂ (≈PaCO₂, ~40 mmHg normal)
  • R: Respiratory exchange ratio (typically 0.8)

Clinical Application: Used to calculate the A-a gradient (Alveolar-arterial oxygen gradient) which helps assess oxygenation efficiency and diagnose respiratory disorders.
